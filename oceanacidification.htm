Ocean Acidification- A Worldwide Threat

caption for video/picture
Image

Ocean acidification – caused by high amounts of atmospheric carbon dioxide dissolving in water – is a serious threat to ecosystems, industry and more. Waters of higher temperatures absorb less amounts of the gas from the atmosphere, which leads to colder water bodies acidifying faster than those in warmer regions. This might lead to the belief that ocean acidification is a local rather than a global threat, but this could not further from the truth! Ocean acidification is present in all sorts of biomes and climates around the world today and is causing significant damage to the industry of countries irrespective of which area of the world they are in. One might consider the cases of India and The Arctic, two countries with climates which could not be more different from each other, but are both experiencing an ongoing struggle against ocean acidification.
